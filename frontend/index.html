TacTight

A Simple 3D-Printed Mechanical System for Reliable Skin-Device Coupling in Wearable Sensors

Reliable skin-device coupling remains a challenge in research involving wearable systems that require consistent mechanical contact with the skin. Currently, researchers often rely on their own subjective perception of how tight a device feels when attaching it to participants, introducing variability and limiting the repeatability of their results. TacTight is a simple, compact, fully 3D-printed system designed to standardize the contact force between wearable devices and the skin using a straightforward visual indicator. All you need is access to a 3D printer!

No sensors. No cables. No electronics.

Context

As researchers experienced with physiological sensors and haptic stimulation devices, we often found ourselves frustrated by the lack of standardized methods for attaching actuators or sensors to a participant's body. Manufacturer instructions offered little clarity, suggesting only vague guidelines such as "not too tight, not too loose," "as tight as is comfortable," or "a snug fit."

The Challenge

These ambiguous instructions, compounded by each user's interpretation, create a situation where there is very little repeatability in how devices are worn, even when the same person installs the device. The issue becomes even more pronounced when multiple people, such as several research assistants, are responsible for installing the systems during data collection.

1

Problem Identification

We explored our options, but apart from integrating load cells into every system we tested, we couldn't find a satisfactory method to make this process more objective.

2

Complexity Challenge

However, adding sensors, electronics, and cabling would have been a much more complex endeavor‚Äînot very practical or appealing unless absolutely crucial for data collection.

The Solution: KISS Principle

With the principle of "KISS‚ÄîKeep It Simple, Stupid" in mind, TacTight (formerly HaptiStrap) was born.

üî¨ What is TacTight?

TacTight is a simple, fully 3D-printed system that you add in series with the bracelet, band, or strap used to apply a sensor or actuator to the user's body. When tightening the band, you simply align the visual indicators on its side, letting you know you are at approximately the intended force.

TacTight installed along the band of a Polar OH1+ optical heart rate sensor.

BUT Here's the Magic

TacTight can be 3D printed in less than 30 minutes using a few cents of PLA, and our evaluations show it significantly improves both the repeatability and accuracy of contact force beneath the bracelet, compared to relying solely on subjective perception of tightness.

4

Attach to Your System

Attach TacTight in series with your bracelet, band, or strap.

5

Position on User

Place the system on the user's body, without tightening it yet.

6

Align Visual Indicators

Tighten the band until the pins on the outer part align with the ring on the main body. When the pins align with the indented circle, the force under TacTight is approximately the value for which it was designed.

Important

Due to variability in 3D printer calibration, PLA materials, and other factors, it is impossible for us to guarantee that your TacTight will exert precisely the intended force when the visual indicator is aligned. However, our system characterization and evaluation with human participants give us confidence that this method provides a more objective and reliable fit than relying solely on subjective perceptions of tightness.
